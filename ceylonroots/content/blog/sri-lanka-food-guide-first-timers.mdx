Sri Lankan food is bold, fragrant, and deeply regional. The same dish can taste different from one town to another.

## What to Try First

- **Rice and curry**: the national staple with multiple side dishes.
- **Hoppers**: bowl-shaped pancakes, often served with egg.
- **Kottu**: chopped roti stir-fried with spices, vegetables, and optional meat.
- **String hoppers**: steamed rice-noodle nests, usually for breakfast.

## Spice and Comfort Tips

- Ask for "mild" if needed; local versions can be hot.
- Coconut sambol is flavorful but often spicy.
- Always keep water and yogurt nearby for balance.

## Best Places to Eat

- Home-style restaurants in Kandy and Galle.
- Family-run cafes near Ella and Nuwara Eliya.
- Seafood-focused beachside spots along the south coast.

## Safe Food Practices

- Choose busy restaurants with high turnover.
- Prefer freshly cooked food over long buffet holds.
- Keep bottled or filtered water with you.

Food is one of the fastest ways to connect with Sri Lanka's culture. Build at least one dedicated food stop into every itinerary.
